Think Like a Scientist

Treat your opinions as hypotheses to be tested with evidence, rather than absolute truths to defend. This mindset encourages openness to new information and adaptability.

  1. Avoid preacher mode: Don't preach beliefs without questioning them.
  2. Avoid prosecutor mode: Don't attack others' views without considering their merit.
  3. Avoid politician mode: Don't shift opinions just to win approval.
  4. Embrace curiosity and seek disconfirming evidence.

Real-World Example: Successful entrepreneurs who test their business ideas like hypotheses earn up to 40 times more by pivoting based on real data rather than sticking to initial assumptions.

Practical Action Plan

  1. Identify one strongly held opinion in your life.
  2. Seek out at least three sources of information that challenge it.
  3. Design a small experiment to test its validity.
  4. Update your view based on the results and reflect on what you learned.

ðŸ“… Day 2: Building Self-Awareness

Confident Humility

Balance confidence in your abilities with humility about your current knowledge, creating a sweet spot for effective rethinking and growth.

  1. Recognize the limits of your expertise.
  2. Maintain confidence in your potential to learn.
  3. Avoid overconfidence that blinds you to new ideas.
  4. Cultivate imposter feelings as a motivator for improvement.

Real-World Example: Forecasters who update their predictions twice as often by admitting uncertainty perform better in accurate predictions during elections.

Practical Action Plan

  1. Assess your confidence in a skill or belief on a scale of 1-10.
  2. List three things you don't know about it.
  3. Seek feedback from others to identify blind spots.
  4. Adjust your approach based on new insights.

ðŸ“… Day 3: Embracing Errors

The Joy of Being Wrong

View mistakes not as failures but as opportunities for discovery and learning, turning being wrong into a positive experience.

  1. Detach your ego from your opinions.
  2. Celebrate discoveries from errors.
  3. Share mistakes openly to normalize learning.
  4. Use errors to refine future decisions.

Real-World Example: Scientists who revise theories based on new evidence, like those in medicine adapting treatments during crises, advance knowledge faster.

Practical Action Plan

  1. Recall a recent mistake you made.
  2. Analyze what led to it without self-judgment.
  3. Identify one lesson learned.
  4. Apply that lesson to a current situation.

ðŸ“… Day 4: Planning Ahead

Rethinking Your Future

Avoid locking into one identity or path early; regularly reassess your goals and directions to adapt to changes.

  1. Conduct regular life checkups.
  2. Avoid identity foreclosure.
  3. Pursue happiness indirectly through meaningful activities.
  4. Be open to career pivots.

Real-World Example: Professionals who switch careers mid-life, like former lawyers becoming teachers, often find greater fulfillment by rethinking their paths.

Practical Action Plan

  1. Schedule a quarterly review of your goals.
  2. List current commitments and question their relevance.
  3. Explore one new interest or skill.
  4. Adjust plans based on reflections.

ðŸ“… Day 5: Interpersonal Dynamics

Constructive Conflict

Use disagreements as opportunities to challenge thinking and foster growth, rather than avoiding or escalating them.

  1. Seek out diverse viewpoints.
  2. Focus on tasks, not personal attacks.
  3. Encourage healthy debate.
  4. Build psychological safety.

Real-World Example: Teams at companies like Pixar that hold "braintrust" meetings to critique ideas constructively, leading to better films.

Practical Action Plan

  1. Identify a current disagreement.
  2. Listen actively to the other side.
  3. Find common ground.
  4. Propose a collaborative solution.

ðŸ“… Day 6: Communication Skills

Debates as Dances

Approach arguments as collaborative dances rather than battles, adjusting to your partner's moves for better persuasion.

  1. Avoid confrontation; seek synchronization.
  2. Use questions to guide thinking.
  3. Highlight nuances over binaries.
  4. Build rapport through empathy.

Real-World Example: Expert negotiators who win deals by finding mutual interests, like in international diplomacy resolving conflicts peacefully.

Practical Action Plan

  1. Prepare for a discussion by listing pros and cons.
  2. Ask open-ended questions.
  3. Acknowledge the other person's points.
  4. Co-create a shared understanding.

ðŸ“… Day 7: Influencing Others

Motivational Interviewing

Use better questions and active listening to help others rethink their views without resistance, guiding them to their own conclusions.

  1. Ask evocative questions.
  2. Reflect back what you hear.
  3. Affirm strengths and efforts.
  4. Summarize key points.

Real-World Example: Doctors using motivational interviewing to encourage patients to quit smoking, resulting in higher success rates.

Practical Action Plan

  1. Choose someone resistant to change.
  2. Ask about their motivations.
  3. Listen without judging.
  4. Help them articulate their own plan.
